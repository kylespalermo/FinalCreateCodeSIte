Landscape-ish Generator

I had the idea to try to work with oil paint-inspired textures based on some image mapping sketches posted on OpenProcessing. Rather than map textures to an existing image, I wanted to create something that would generate an abstract, impressionist-like composition of primitive shapes, drawn from a canvas of strokes, rather than actually rendering any ellipses. The visual inspiration comes from the colors and landscapes in the Diablo Range near San Jose, California, where I grew up and have passed some time this summer.

A more interactive version

Here, you can take a little more control over the composition by planting circles with a mouse click.

A more pyschadellic version

A little more free-form, this version plants circles randomly, with strokes and shapes cycling through the color wheel and dying circles spawning two more in their place.
